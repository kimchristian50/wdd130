Our Rafting Trips:

The Rio Grande Racecourse half-day trip is perfect for first-time rafters or people with limited time. Morning trips meet at 8:00am and afternoon trips meet at 12:30pm at our headquarters, where you will board vans to get to our pushing-off point. You will enjoy Class II-III (IV at high water) rapids, along with beautiful scenery. Snacks are provided, and lunch can be added as an extra.

The Rio Grande Racecourse full-day trip begins at 8:30am at our headquarters, where you will meet your guides for the day. After a short van ride, your river trip begins with a calm and scenic ride through the canyon with opportunities to view birds and wildlife and practice paddling. After a picnic lunch by the side of the river, you will enjoy the Class II-III rapids of the Racecourse. Lunch and snacks are provided.

The Rio Chama 3 day trip consists of a relaxed 3-day itinerary where we stop and explore hot springs, ancient ruins, and dinosaur tracks. We camp for two nights under the bright New Mexican sky on the sandy river beaches. There will be time for hiking and exploring in this beautiful area. We will float past the colorful sandstone and limestone walls of the canyon and finish up near the artist village of Abiquiu. All camping gear and meals will be provided.

The Rio Grande Float half-day trip begins at 9:00am at our headquarter office. After a short van ride, you will enjoy a relaxing float through a scenic canyon with great opportunities to see wildlife and many different types of water birds and birds of prey. This is the perfect trip for anyone who needs some relaxing time to just float amongst the beautiful canyon scenery. The float takes about 3 hours. Snacks are provided.

The Taos Box full-day trip begins at 8:30am and is usually finished by 3:00pm, depending on the water levels. This river run through the heart of the Rio Grande Gorge begins relatively calmly with plenty of opportunities to work on your paddling techniques. About half-way through, a delicious riverside lunch will be served. After lunch, you will enjoy the Class IV whitewater rapids throughout the last five miles of the trip. You will paddle with a team with a guide sitting in back to steer you safely through the rapids.

For confident kayakers, the Taos Box kayak trip offers the opportunity to paddle your own boat on the middle Taos Box section. You will pack an inflatable kayak to the confluence of the Red River and the Rio Grande and will get all the instruction you need from your guides. The whitewater on this section is calm with one class III rapid. We will serve you lunch on the beach and, after a short hike, you can see some riverside archeology sites.
