Pam Christison | WDD 130

I'm a mom, a chemical engineer, a patent analyst, and I'm studying Computer Science. My family and I love to travel, create, and love being members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Right now I am a seminary teacher, and I love getting to spend time with my co-teacher husband and the students every morning at 6:10am. I'm also the ward organist and have the powerful ability to choose the music every Sunday. My family and I most recently traveled to Scandinavia, which was an amazing trip where we saw castles, cathedrals, art, former military installations, fjords, and lots of waterfalls. New Mexico is low on waterfalls, in particular near the Albuquerque area where we live, so we especially enjoyed all the water. One highlight for our family was that we were able to visit the Copenhagen temple. Other favorite places were the Viking museum in Roskilde, the Viking village Njardarheimr in Gudvangen, and the Vasa warship restoration museum in Stockholm. We were also able to take the overnight ferry/cruise between Helsinki and Stockholm and see the beautiful archipelago as we neared Sweden. This picture was taken in Hovdestøylen, a ski resort area in Norway.

New Mexico

New Mexico is a southwestern U.S. state known for its striking desert and mountain landscapes, rich Native American and Hispanic heritage, and long, layered history. It is home to diverse geography ranging from high desert mesas and red rock formations to forested mountains. The state also plays an important role in science and national defense, with major research institutions and national laboratories, while maintaining a strong connection to tradition, land, and community.
